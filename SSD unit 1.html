Secure Software Development Unit 1

Reflection

Interesting to see the different approaches used for software development – the Agile approach lets developers go back to earlier stages of the life cycle without any major difficulty, whereas the Waterfall approach is a step-by-step method and so any need to go back steps can result in significant implications. A further approach called the Spiral method combines the iterative process (i.e. going back after doing something and adjusting it like a loop) with the step-by-step process of the Waterfall model, which minimises the weakness of the waterfall approach.

Waterfall model – everyone has a specific role and waits until “their turn” – tasks are very rigid based on their job title

Agile – everyone works on everything – people work collaboratively (e.g. paired programming) – very fluid process

Interesting and insightful blog about the development of the different approaches/frameworks (Agile, Waterfall, Scrum) in software development and their strengths/weaknesses. It seems that the right (and most common) solution is to apply the most appropriate model to the kind of problem that you have rather that just using one approach for everything.
