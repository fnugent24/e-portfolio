Collaborative Tools

The team used a range of tools during the module and the summative assignments. We were able to collaborate on ideas and thoughts for how to progress as a team and share our own experiences and opinions as well as provide structure for tasks.

Miro:

The team agreed to use a tool called Miro for the purposes of tracking ideas, design thoughts, high level requirements and general ideas. We used this as a platform to bring about functional and non-functional requirements which were then recorded in a separate tool. It also allowed us to work together in real time without the need for meetings while providing us a way to review efforts on a regular basis.

Trello:

The team decided to adopt Trello, which offered us a board similar to a Jira scrum or agile board. This lightweight tool provided a straightforward and efficient means of setting reminders, organising tasks, and managing deadlines. It greatly supported our development process by enabling the team to smoothly advance through typical sprint behaviours.

GitHub:

The team agreed to use a single GitHub repository for the code base for the development project/activities. By using a shared repository, we were able to independently work on development activities. The repository can be found at:

https://github.com/sbolder77/SSD_Team_Project
